Stories of Change

Real impact in the lives of communities we serve

At SANKET, we believe in the power of stories to inspire, connect, and drive change. The stories shared here are testament to the resilience of communities and individuals who, with support and opportunity, have transformed their lives and the world around them.

Each narrative represents countless hours of dedication, collaboration, and perseverance. They showcase not just what SANKET has accomplished, but what communities can achieve when empowered with the right resources and support.

As you explore these stories, we invite you to see beyond the statistics and connect with the human experiences that drive our mission forward.

Featured Story

From Struggle to Strength: The Women of Rajgarh Village

In the remote village of Rajgarh, a group of 15 women have transformed not just their own lives but the economic landscape of their entire community. What began as a small skill development workshop by SANKET in 2019 has evolved into a thriving cooperative that produces and sells handcrafted textiles to markets across India and internationally.

The cooperative now generates sustainable income for over 50 families, has funded a community center, and sponsors education for girls in the village. Most importantly, it has shifted perceptions about women's roles and capabilities in a traditionally patriarchal setting.

Before SANKET came to our village, I had never earned money in my life. Today, I am the financial decision-maker in my family and have sent both my daughters to college. We didn't just learn to make products; we learned to believe in ourselves.

Sunita Devi

Cooperative Chairperson

Voices from the Community

Hear directly from those whose lives have been touched

Ramesh Singh

Farmer, Marathwada

Before SANKET's agricultural program, I was considering leaving farming altogether. The drought had made it impossible to earn a living. Now, with organic methods and water conservation techniques, my farm is thriving even in dry years. My children who had left for the city are now returning to join the family business.

Lakshmi Kumari

Student, Delhi

The digital literacy program changed my life completely. I was the first in my family to use a computer, and now I'm studying computer science at university. The mentors at SANKET didn't just teach me technical skills; they showed me that someone from my background could succeed in the tech world.

Arjun Patel

Village Head, Uttarakhand

Our village had been losing its youth to migration for decades. Since partnering with SANKET on the river restoration project, we've seen a revival not just of our environment but of our community spirit. Young people are finding meaningful work here, and our traditional knowledge is being valued and preserved alongside modern conservation techniques.
